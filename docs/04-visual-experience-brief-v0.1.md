# Complete Cruising

## Visual Experience Brief v0.1

### Product context

**Complete Cruising** is a Lawrence Family Series application designed to become a personalised, visually rich cruise companion.

The product should combine itinerary intelligence, port guidebook content, ship knowledge, family planning, weather awareness, Seb-focused discovery and memory capture.

This brief defines the intended visual and interaction experience.

The aim is not to create a basic travel utility. The aim is to create an outstanding cruise application that feels premium, immersive, practical and emotionally connected to the experience of a luxury family sailing.

---

# 1. Purpose of this brief

This visual experience brief defines how Complete Cruising should look and feel before the first standalone HTML concept prototype is produced.

The brief should guide:

- visual identity
    
- colour palette
    
- typography
    
- layout structure
    
- card system
    
- screen concepts
    
- interaction design
    
- responsive behaviour
    
- accessibility expectations
    
- prototype direction
    
- future PWA design
    

The design should make the user feel they are opening a bespoke cruise companion, not a generic admin tool.

# 3. Design statement

**Complete Cruising should feel like a luxury ocean voyage translated into an interactive digital guidebook.**

It should combine:

- deep ocean atmosphere
    
- sunlit destination warmth
    
- elegant cruise-line polish
    
- practical operational clarity
    
- family adventure energy
    
- guidebook-style curation
    
- subtle nautical detail
    

The visual design should be rich, but not cluttered. It should be premium, but still friendly. It should be practical, but never plain.

# 4. Design principles

## 4.1 Premium before playful

The app can include fun elements for Seb, but the overall product should feel premium and refined.

The child-friendly layer should sit within the design elegantly, not make the app feel like a children’s product.

## 4.2 Beautiful but useful

Every decorative element should support the cruise mood or make information easier to understand.

Avoid decoration that competes with the content.

## 4.3 Cruise-specific throughout

The product should use cruise language, cruise metaphors and cruise visual motifs.

Examples:

- voyage
    
- sailing
    
- port call
    
- sea day
    
- ashore
    
- all aboard
    
- embarkation
    
- disembarkation
    
- ship guide
    
- horizon
    
- route
    
- compass
    
- deck
    
- terminal
    
- tender
    

## 4.4 Guidebook, not database

The app should present enriched information like a curated guidebook.

Even when data is structured, the interface should not look like a spreadsheet.

## 4.5 Today must be instantly useful

The Today view should be the clearest, most operational screen.

It should answer:

- where are we?
    
- what time matters?
    
- what are we doing?
    
- what should we take?
    
- what might go wrong?
    
- what should Seb notice?
    

## 4.6 Confidence should be visible but elegant

Review states, uncertainty and refresh warnings should be clear without making the app look technical or alarming.

## 4.7 Mobile-first, tablet-enhanced, desktop-beautiful

The app must work well on a phone, especially onboard and ashore.

Larger screens should feel more cinematic and immersive.

- labels
    
- buttons
    
- chips
    
- dashboard metrics

### Data type

Use a compact, highly readable style for times, weather and itinerary details.

Purpose:

- arrival
    
- departure
    
- all aboard
    
- temperature
    
- day number
    
- countdown
    

## 9.2 Typographic personality

The app should feel editorial and precise.

Titles can be expressive. Operational details must be clear.

- Large cinematic title: “Sun Princess Mediterranean”
    
- Subtitle: “Rome to Barcelona”
    
- Operational line: “Day 4 - Souda Bay - All aboard 17:30”

# 15. Status and confidence visual language

The app must show data confidence clearly.

## 15.1 Confidence chip mapping

|Confidence|Visual treatment|
|---|---|
|Confirmed|Teal or green-blue chip|
|High|Blue-teal chip|
|Medium|Gold chip|
|Low|Amber chip|
|Inferred|Grey-blue chip|
|Unknown|Muted grey chip|

## 15.2 Review status mapping

|Status|Visual treatment|
|---|---|
|Reviewed|Small tick|
|Verified|Stronger confirmed badge|
|Needs review|Gold outline|
|Needs refresh|Circular arrow|
|Stale|Coral warning|
|Rejected|Muted red or hidden unless needed|

## 15.3 Warning rule

Warnings should be calm and useful.

Avoid harsh red unless the issue is genuinely significant.

Example:

“Terminal details need refreshing before travel.”

Better than:

“Warning: unreliable data.”

# 18. Accessibility and readability

Visual richness must not compromise usability.

## 18.1 Contrast

Text contrast must remain strong, especially over gradients or textured backgrounds.

Use overlays where needed.

## 18.2 Text size

Operational information should be large enough to read quickly on mobile.

Critical information includes:

- all-aboard time
    
- departure time
    
- selected plan
    
- warnings
    
- weather impact
    

## 18.3 Colour independence

Status should not rely on colour alone.

Use labels and icons as well.

## 18.4 Motion

Animations should be subtle and optional.

Avoid effects that may distract from operational use.

## 18.5 Offline clarity

The app should clearly show when information was last refreshed.

Example:

“Forecast last updated 2 days ago.”

# 39. Risks and mitigations

## Risk 1: The app becomes visually overdecorated

Mitigation:

Use subtle nautical motifs and preserve clear hierarchy.

## Risk 2: The app looks like a generic dashboard

Mitigation:

Use cruise-specific language, route visuals, port postcards, ship motifs and guidebook sections.

## Risk 3: The app becomes hard to use on mobile

Mitigation:

Design Today and Itinerary mobile-first, with collapsible detail.

## Risk 4: The family layer feels childish

Mitigation:

Keep Seb discovery visually warm but contained within the premium design system.

## Risk 5: Confidence metadata clutters the UI

Mitigation:

Use small chips, expandable confidence notes and calm warning cards.

# 41. v0.1 conclusion

Complete Cruising should deliver a premium cruise experience through design as well as data.

The app should feel oceanic, elegant, practical and personal. It should create anticipation before the cruise, confidence during the cruise, and warmth after the cruise.

The strongest visual direction is a luxury maritime guidebook blended with an onboard command centre.

The design must make space for rich enrichment, beautiful port discovery, operational timing, family relevance and memory capture.

Complete Cruising should not merely display cruise data.

**It should make the sailing feel alive before the family ever steps aboard.**
