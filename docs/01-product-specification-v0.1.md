## 1. Product vision

**Complete Cruising** is a visually rich, personalised cruise companion designed to transform a cruise booking into a detailed, practical and beautiful living guidebook.

The application should help the Lawrence family understand each sailing before travel, navigate each cruise day with confidence, and preserve meaningful memories afterwards.

The product should combine the feel of:

- a luxury cruise brochure
    
- a premium travel journal
    
- a port guidebook
    
- a ship handbook
    
- a family itinerary planner
    
- a maritime atlas
    
- an onboard daily companion
    

It should not feel like a generic travel database. It should feel unmistakably cruise-specific, oceanic, elegant and fun.

The ambition is to build the ultimate personalised cruise layer: a trusted pocket guide that understands the ship, the ports, the itinerary, the family, the risks, the weather, the opportunities and the memories being created.

---

## 2. Product purpose

The purpose of Complete Cruising is to help the family:

1. Prepare intelligently for a cruise holiday.
    
2. Understand the ship before boarding.
    
3. Explore each port with richer context.
    
4. Make better decisions while ashore.
    
5. Keep practical cruise details close at hand.
    
6. Refresh information as the sailing approaches.
    
7. Create a more exciting, educational and memorable experience for Seb.
    
8. Capture completed cruise experiences for future reflection.
    
9. Export selected memories and travel facts into Adventure Almanac.
    

The application should reduce uncertainty, increase anticipation, and provide a sense of control during a complex, moving holiday.

Complete Cruising should model this properly.

Each sailing includes:

- the cruise booking
    
- the ship
    
- the cabin
    
- the cruise line
    
- the itinerary
    
- each port
    
- each day’s timing
    
- shore plans
    
- onboard plans
    
- travel documents
    
- packing considerations
    
- weather
    
- local context
    
- family preferences

## 7. Core user journeys

### Journey 1: Create a new sailing

The user creates a new sailing record with:

- cruise line
    
- ship
    
- sailing name
    
- departure date
    
- return date
    
- embarkation port
    
- disembarkation port
    
- cabin details
    
- travellers
    
- booking reference
    
- package notes
    
- linked travel arrangements
    

The initial record may be sparse. Enrichment can happen later.

### Journey 2: Build the itinerary shell

The user adds or imports the day-by-day itinerary:

- day number
    
- date
    
- port or sea day
    
- arrival time
    
- departure time
    
- all-aboard time
    
- tender or docked
    
- notes
    
- confidence level
    

The itinerary shell becomes the spine of the whole application.

### Journey 3: Enrich the ship

The ship is enriched through multiple isolated research passes.

Ship enrichment should be split into separate sections such as:

- ship identity
    
- ship layout
    
- dining
    
- cabins
    
- pools and recreation
    
- family facilities
    
- entertainment
    
- quiet spaces
    
- practical tips
    
- premium/luxury experience
    
- watchouts
    
- photography spots
    

Each section should be generated, reviewed and imported separately.

### Journey 4: Enrich each port

Each port is enriched through targeted, modular passes.

Port enrichment should be split into:

- fact file
    
- cruise logistics
    
- getting around
    
- top attractions
    
- family suitability
    
- hints and tips
    
- food and drink
    
- local culture
    
- photography
    
- weather and seasonality
    
- risks and return buffer
    
- suggested shore plans
    

Each enrichment section should have its own source confidence and review status.

### Journey 5: Generate a day guide

Once the sailing, itinerary, ship and port records are sufficiently enriched, the app should generate a curated day guide for each cruise day.

A day guide should answer:

- Where are we today?
    
- What time do we arrive?
    
- What time must we return?
    
- What is the likely weather?
    
- What is the family plan?
    
- What is the backup plan?
    
- What should we take ashore?
    
- What should Seb notice or learn?
    
- What local phrase might be useful?
    
- What photo should we try to capture?
    
- What is the return-to-ship risk?
    

### Journey 6: Use the Today view onboard

During the cruise, the Today view should become the primary interface.

It should show:

- today’s date and day number
    
- current port or sea day
    
- port times
    
- all-aboard time
    
- weather
    
- local currency
    
- local language
    
- planned activity
    
- backup option
    
- ashore checklist
    
- key warnings
    
- family notes
    
- Seb’s discovery card
    
- memory prompt
    

This view should feel highly polished and immediately useful.

### Journey 7: Capture memories

After each day or at the end of the cruise, the user can capture:

- what we did
    
- what we enjoyed
    
- what surprised us
    
- best photo
    
- Seb’s favourite part
    
- food tried
    
- things to remember
    
- whether the plan worked
    
- whether the port deserves a future return
    

### Journey 8: Export to Adventure Almanac

Completed cruise data should be exportable to Adventure Almanac.

The export should include:

- confirmed cruise dates
    
- ship
    
- cruise line
    
- embarkation/disembarkation ports
    
- actual ports visited
    
- countries visited
    
- travel companions
    
- memories
    
- highlights
    
- photos to attach later
    
- educational notes for Seb
    

Complete Cruising should be the planning and onboard intelligence layer. Adventure Almanac remains the long-term travel record.

## 11. Enrichment philosophy

Complete Cruising should use targeted enrichment, not broad enrichment.

Large prompts are likely to produce shallow, blended or overconfident content. Accuracy improves when research is split into small, well-defined jobs.

### Enrichment principles

1. Enrich one object at a time.
    
2. Enrich one aspect at a time.
    
3. Keep factual, practical and subjective guidance separate.
    
4. Preserve confidence levels.
    
5. Track when content was last reviewed.
    
6. Mark time-sensitive content as refreshable.
    
7. Never treat inferred information as confirmed.
    
8. Allow manual correction.
    
9. Keep enrichment importable as structured JSON.
    
10. Prefer usefulness over completeness.
    

### Enrichment status values

- not_started
    
- draft
    
- imported
    
- reviewed
    
- verified
    
- needs_refresh
    
- stale
    
- rejected
    

### Confidence levels

- confirmed
    
- high
    
- medium
    
- low
    
- inferred
    
- unknown
    

### Source type values

- cruise_line_confirmed
    
- booking_confirmed
    
- official_port_source
    
- official_tourism_source
    
- reputable_travel_source
    
- researched
    
- family_note
    
- inferred
    
- user_entered

## 13. Weather and refresh model

Weather should be treated as refreshable intelligence.

The app should separate:

- seasonal expectation
    
- medium-range forecast
    
- short-range forecast
    
- same-day conditions
    

### Weather states

- climate_only
    
- forecast_pending
    
- forecast_available
    
- forecast_recent
    
- forecast_stale
    
- same_day_checked
    

### Suggested refresh windows

- 90+ days before sailing: seasonal climate only
    
- 30 days before sailing: packing expectations
    
- 16 days before port visit: initial forecast
    
- 7 days before port visit: practical planning forecast
    
- 48 hours before port visit: operational forecast
    
- day of visit: final check
    

The app should make clear when weather data is old or approximate.

## 15. Interaction principles

The app should be enjoyable, but it must also remain practical.

### Interaction rules

1. The Today view should require minimal scrolling.
    
2. The itinerary should be visually scannable.
    
3. Port records can be rich, but day guides must be curated.
    
4. Confidence warnings should be visible but not ugly.
    
5. Manual edits should always be possible.
    
6. Imported content should be reviewable before commit.
    
7. No screen should feel like a raw database table.
    
8. Cruise-specific language should be used throughout.
    
9. The app should work well on phone and tablet.
    
10. Offline access should be considered important for onboard use.

## 16. Offline and onboard considerations

Cruise holidays can involve poor mobile signal, expensive Wi-Fi, unreliable roaming and shipboard connectivity limits.

Complete Cruising should eventually support offline-first behaviour.

### MVP expectation

- Core sailing data available offline once loaded
    
- Itinerary available offline
    
- Port guides available offline
    
- Ship guide available offline
    
- Documents checklist available offline
    
- Manual notes saved locally
    
- Weather requires refresh when connected
    

### Future expectation

- Full PWA install
    
- Offline cache status
    
- “Ready for sea” indicator
    
- Last sync timestamp
    
- Exportable backup
    
- Conflict-safe manual edits

## 21. Success criteria

Complete Cruising is successful if it:

1. Makes the family more excited before the cruise.
    
2. Makes the ship easier to understand before boarding.
    
3. Makes each port easier to evaluate.
    
4. Helps the family choose realistic plans ashore.
    
5. Reduces uncertainty around timing, transport and return buffers.
    
6. Gives Seb a richer learning experience.
    
7. Feels beautiful and premium enough to match the holiday context.
    
8. Works without depending on live connectivity at sea.
    
9. Keeps enriched content reviewable and refreshable.
    
10. Produces useful memories and exports after the trip.
    

The highest success signal is simple:

**On the cruise, the family naturally opens Complete Cruising each morning because it gives them a better day.**

## 22. Product risks

### Risk 1: Data becomes too broad

Mitigation:

- use targeted enrichment packs
    
- keep day guides curated
    
- avoid encyclopaedic port records
    

### Risk 2: AI enrichment sounds confident but is wrong

Mitigation:

- confidence metadata
    
- source type tracking
    
- review status
    
- refresh flags
    
- manual correction
    

### Risk 3: UI becomes admin-heavy

Mitigation:

- prioritise visual cards
    
- hide management actions behind import/edit areas
    
- make Explore and Today views beautiful
    

### Risk 4: It tries to replace the cruise line app

Mitigation:

- focus on family intelligence, not official booking management
    
- do not manage onboard reservations directly in MVP
    

### Risk 5: Scope becomes too large

Mitigation:

- start with one sailing
    
- build depth before breadth
    
- prove Sun Princess before importing cruise history

## 23. Product judgement

Complete Cruising is justified because cruise holidays create a unique information problem.

A family cruise involves:

- a moving itinerary
    
- limited time ashore
    
- changing weather
    
- unfamiliar ports
    
- ship facilities
    
- onboard schedules
    
- excursion decisions
    
- family preferences
    
- documents and logistics
    
- memories worth capturing
    

Generic travel apps do not handle this well. Notes apps are not structured enough. Cruise line apps are not personal enough. Guidebooks are not itinerary-aware. Weather apps are not cruise-contextual. Spreadsheets are not enjoyable.

Complete Cruising should occupy that missing space.

It should become the personalised cruise companion the family wishes existed.

Complete Cruising should be developed as a premium, visually immersive, enrichment-led cruise companion for the Lawrence Family Series.
